Legal aspects

Governments around the world have enacted a myriad of laws to combat the illicit use, trafficking, and possession of controlled substances. Penalties for drug-related offenses vary widely but are uniformly aimed at dissuading and penalizing those who flout these regulations.

Control of Narcotic Substances Act, 1997:

Section 6(b): Unlawful trafficking or financing of trafficking. Punishment: A minimum of six months' imprisonment and a fine.

Control of Narcotic Substances Act, 1997:

Section 9(c): Possession of controlled substances more than allowed Punishment:Rigorous imprisonment for a term which may extend to seven years and a fine.

Control of Narcotic Substances Act, 1997:

Section 15:Offenses related to the financing of illicit traffic. Punishment:Rigorous imprisonment for a term not less than fourteen years but may extend to life imprisonment and a fine.
