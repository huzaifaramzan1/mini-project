Drug control refers to the policies, laws, and actions implemented by governments and international organizations to regulate the production, distribution, and consumption of controlled substances. This encompasses both legal and illegal drugs, aiming to prevent misuse and abuse while ensuring that legitimate medical and scientific needs are met.
Significance:
1- Public Health Protection: Effective drug control policies help protect public health by reducing the prevalence of drug addiction, preventing overdoses, and minimizing the spread of drug-related diseases such as HIV/AIDS and hepatitis.
2- Crime Reduction: Drug control measures contribute to a decrease in drug-related crimes, including trafficking, violence, and organized crime activities. By disrupting illegal drug markets, law enforcement agencies can reduce criminal activities associated with drug trade.
3-Youth Safety: Stringent drug control helps protect young people from the dangers of substance abuse. By regulating the availability and distribution of drugs, these policies aim to reduce the exposure and accessibility of harmful substances to minors.

Drug Abuse Approximately 275 million people worldwide used drugs in the past year.

Youth Drug Use Around 13% of the global drug-using population are young people aged 15-24.

Drug-Related Deaths 500,000 deaths per year
