INTERNATIONAL

COOPERATION

International cooperation on drug control is paramount in addressing the complex and interconnected challenges posed by illicit drug trafficking and abuse across borders. Recognizing that the drug trade knows no boundaries, countries around the world have joined forces to establish collaborative frameworks and initiatives aimed at preventing the production, trafficking, and consumption of illegal substances. Through international organizations such as the United Nations Office on Drugs and Crime (UNODC) , Interpol, and regional partnerships, nations share information, coordinate enforcement efforts, and implement strategies to disrupt drug supply chains.
